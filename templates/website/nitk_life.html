     INSIDE NITK-FACILITIES


    Silver Jubilee Auditorium



    SJA is a huge auditorium that regularly hosts major events of NITK. Formed to commemorate NITK's Silver Jubilee, it is the projection of NITK’s pride as one of the top engineering institutions in the country.
    The SJA has a capacity of around 1000. It is fully air conditioned and also has a balcony housing. SJA is decked up for all major occasions and looks rather stunning during the nights. As a fresher one should not miss the musical nights. Those are truly performances worth cherishing (and the AC worth sitting for!). Films are regularly screened by the Films’ Club and block timings are extended during the screening of these films!
    All the fests start with their inaugural functions at the SJA, be it ENGINEER, INCIDENT or Crrescendo for that matter. The crowd particularly goes in a frenzy whenever there is an amazing performance and it is a treat to watch especially from the balcony.
    The building is centrally located in the campus just adjacent to Ocean Pearl and opposite to Central Computer Centre(CCC).



    Students' Activity Center (SAC)


    Students Activity Centre is the arena right beside the HCC and in front of the basketball court. It's quite a serene and calm place on a normal day. It is often used as a popular hangout place by most people during the evenings.
    Come the fests and this place will be busy buzzing with students as all the major performances and events take place here. Every night during INCI and two of the nights during ENGINEER this place is filled with people waiting for the band events or the DJ Show.
    And that’s not it, Films and plays will be regularly screened and sometimes cricket matches are also shown. During the Indian cricket team’s matches the entire college comes over here to cheer and it provides the thrill that one experiences in a stadium. A great atmosphere makes this place awesome during the movie screenings.



    Library


    The NITK Library is housed in a spacious two-storied independent building adjoining the Main building of the Institute on the Eastern side of the campus. The library is segregated into two wings.Periodicals, journals and bound volumes are accessible in the Western wing, while books are housed in the Eastern wing.
    The Reading halls provides the peaceful and friendly atmosphere for studying peacefully.There is also an AC room to find respite from the sweltering Surathkal heat. The library also has a Xerox and printing facility on the 1st floor along with a facility to read newspapers in various languages. The second floor has a Reading Room which can be used by students to study in groups or for discussing a particular project/ assignment.
    A computer terminal is provided which can be used to gain information regarding availability and particulars of any book. A bar-coded system of issue and return of books is currently in use. Laptops are allowed inside the library and e-books or research papers can be downloaded from the library Wi-Fi.

    Health Care Centre


    The Health Care Centre also known as HCC is a unique facility. Free doctors + medicine! Many private colleges do not have such a facility. It is situated in the centre of the campus opposite the main grounds.
    There are doctors available at the given timings and all you need to show is your ID Card to get free treatment as well as free medicines from the in-house pharmacy. Specialist doctors visit the campus once a week and you can book an appointment with them at the campus itself. NITK has recently inducted a 24-hour ambulance for emergencies.
    You will also be covered under a medical insurance by HCC, NITK. It's available throughout Karnataka in almost all major hospitals :P.You can claim lakhs of money in case you undergo some medical treatment in and around Mangalore. You can even get rebate in consultancy fees of top-notch doctors in almost all the Hospitals in Mangalore.



    Main Building


    This is the mother of all buildings at NITK. Here lies the Administration office and all the officials’ office. From the Dean to the Director, everyone’s office can be found here. Visit this place for all official purposes. ( A word of warning, don’t enter this building in shorts or you are done for :P)
    There are two seminar halls in this building namely - Main Seminar Hall (MSH) and the ISTE seminar hall. Regular seminars and (boring :P) quizzes take place here.
    There are 5 classrooms on the ground floor which are often used by students for self-study or for clubs to hold their meetings and to conduct events for the first-years.
    And then lastly there is a hotel called Samudra Darshan on the top floor where you can have regular breakfast/lunch. The panoramic view from there is one not to be missed.



    Central Computer Centre


    CCC (pronounced trip-see) occupies the ground and first floor of the building opposite to the Silver Jubilee Auditorium. CCC was established in 1995 as a service providing/supporting facility that augments to the computing facilities in the teaching departments.
    There is a computer lab on the ground floor which is accessed only by first years for their Computer Programming classes. However on the first floor there is another lab which is used by the students and is open to all for their personal use. CCC is also responsible for Internet connectivity throughout campus and all complaints for internet connections not working in your rooms are addressed here.

    NITK Hostel


    The Institute is a completely residential campus with hostel facility for over 5000 students including separate blocks for girls. Hostel blocks have reading rooms and separate dining halls which serve different cuisine. All rooms in hostels have been provided with high speed broadband and Wi-Fi internet connectivity. The accommodation is single-seater and 2/3 seater shared type. A separate hostel for the Post Graduate students is available. To suit the tastes of the students from different regions, independent messes of various types are run in different hostels. Moreover two night canteens are running in boys as well as in girls’ premises to cater the late night hunger. Moreover five well established cafeterias are there in different corner of the campus to caters the hunger with delicious and hygienic food items.



    NITK Guest House




    The institute has 3 Guest Houses and one International Hostel with state of the art infrastructure which provides accommodation to Institute guests, staff and guardians of the students.

     SPORTS IN NITK


    Did you think that NITK is just a temple of nerds and extracurricular activities like sports and games are not given any kind of attention then think again. NITK offers state of the art sports facilities. The college sports an Olympic size swimming pool, three tennis courts, two basketball courts, two volleyball courts, full size football and cricket grounds, one hockey field, one handball court, three badminton courts, five T.T tables etc. Other games like Chess and Carrom are also encouraged.

    Swimming Pool



    All students are welcome to register and utilize the facility. But being in a proper swimming attire is a must i.e. swimming costume/trunks + cap. Instructor and life guards are always present. But the registration process is a bit of a pain; the student has to submit a Xerox copy of his/her admission letter in order to get the registration form.
    Then he/she has to get an approval from a doctor in Health Care Centre saying that he/she is fit for swimming. Swimming wear can be brought by the student or it can also be bought in the store inside the swimming pool. Advice: Maximize its use in summer.



    Football


    Football is very popular in NITK. Every evening one can find at least 6-8 groups playing football in the ground apart from the college team. Footballs and practice cones can be issued from the sports complex adjacent to the ground by giving your college ID (or Library card). Again Inter collegiate and Inter year tournaments are held. Also there is a special “only freshers” tournament held in the beginning of 1st semester between the twelve 1st year classes. Five a side Beach football is organized during Incident and five a side ‘flood lit’ football tournament is held during Phoenix.
    Inter NIT tournament was held last year and NITK won the Gold medal. Want to get into the college team? Just practice with the team every evening. Selections will be held in the 1st semester.



    Basketball


    Basketball: There are two basketball courts. Both of them are in a great condition. One of them is reserved for the college team and should be vacated whenever they come for practice. The other one can be made use of at any time. There are two major competitions that take place on these courts, one is the inter collegiate basketball tournament known as the “Slam dunk” which takes place during Incident, and the other one is the inter year basketball tournament which takes place during crescendo.
    Inter class freshers tournament is held in the 1st and want to get into the college team just join the team in practice every evening. Selections will be held.



    Cricket


    Cricket too is very popular in NITK. Cricket bats, balls, stumps etc. can be issued from the complex by giving your college ID. Inter Year and Inter branch tournaments are held. Last year the inter class “mixed” cricket tournament was organized (5 boys + 2 girls required in one team :P). Selections for getting into the College cricket team will be held in either the 1st or 2nd semester.



    Badminton/Table Tennis


    The courts and tables are located inside the sports complex. The complex is open from 6 in the morning to 8 in the evening. One can issue T.T balls and Shuttles from the complex.
    But one has to bring their own T.T bats and Badminton racquets too play. Inter class badminton and T.T tournament is held at the beginning of 1st semester for the freshers by the Recreational committee.

     ACADEMICS


    • Each academic year shall be divided into 2 semesters, each of 20 weeks duration, including evaluation and grade finalization, etc. The Academic Session in each semester shall provide for at least 70 Teaching Days, with at least 40 hours of teaching contact periods in a five-days session per week. The semester that is typically from Mid July to November is called the ODD SEMESTER, and the one that is from January to Mid-May is called the EVEN SEMESTER.
    • Academic Session may be scheduled for the Summer Session/Semester as well.
    • The normal duration of the course leading to B.Tech. degree will be EIGHT semesters.
    • The normal duration of the course leading to M.Tech. degree will be FOUR semesters
    • The normal duration of the course leading to M.C.A. degree will be SIX semesters.
    • The normal duration of the course leading to M.B.A. degree will be FOUR semesters
    • The normal duration of the course leading to M.Sc. degree will be FOUR semesters


    NITK Evaluation System:

    • Course Credit Assignment: 
      Every Course comprises of specific Lecture-Tutorial-Practical (L-T-P) Schedule. The Course Credits are fixed based on
    the following norms:
               Lectures/ Tutorials :            One hour per week is assigned one Credit.
               Practicals :                    (i) a 3-hour session per week is assigned two Credits;
                                                                 OR
                                               (ii) a 2-hour session per week is assigned one Credit.

For example, a theory course with a L-T-P schedule of 3-1-0 will be assigned 4 credits; a
laboratory practical course with a L-T-P schedule of 0-0-3 will be assigned 2 credits.
    • The Academic Performance Evaluation of a Student shall be according to a Letter Grading System, based on the Class Performance Distribution, and not based upon any fixed apriori mappings or any absolute scale conversions from the Raw-Scores Scale (e.g. percentage-marks) to the Grade- Points Scale.
    • The double-letter grade (AA, AB, BB, BC, CC, CD, DD, FF) indicates the level of academic achievement, assessed on a decimal (0-10) scale.
